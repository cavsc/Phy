Welcome to eKPI

Protein phosphorylation is one of the most widely studied post-translational modification which regulates cell growth, differentiation, apoptosis, signaling and many other cellular functions. Alterations in phosphorylation result in serious outcomes in the form of diseases, especially cancer. With the development of deep learning, more and more models have been applied to predict phosphorylation sites. With the rapid development of proteomics technology, a large number of phosphorylated proteome data sets and clinical multiomics data have been generated. Thus, a comprehensive one-stop data resource for surfing the big data will be very useful to the community.
